Task 2

Comprehension questions 1. Why are people today dissatisfied with higher education?(para. 1) Parents think the tuition fees are too high; students find that university degrees do not add much value to them at the job market; and employers complain that college graduates are not job-ready. 2. What did Ronald Reagan think of the purpose of highereducation? (para. 3) Reagan did not think universities were a place for a handful of scholars to practice knowledge for knowledge’s sake (i.e. to explore subjects that are not practically useful but only satisfy their intellectual curiosity) because public universities are supported with taxpayers’ money. 3. What do colleges do to boost enrollment? (para. 6) Colleges that are eager to recruit students compete to introduce new majors in occupational and vocational areas to attract more students to enroll. 4. What does Michael Roth mean by saying that the currentcomplaints of the employers about new grads is a “manu-factured moment”? (para. 8) What he means is that the dissatisfaction of employers with newly grads is not a new thing. He argues that the employers have always been so and why the problem is so manifest is that today media report too much about it 5. Who is to blame for the disconnect between college and the Questions workplace according to Roth? (para. 16) First, parents, students and employers are to blame, according to Roth, for they are simply expecting too much about higher education. Second, economy is not offering enough opportunities for young people and people are anxious about how they would make a decent living after graduation. Third, colleges are to blame as well, for they are charging too much for the education.

Instead of teaching knowledge for knowledge’s sake, Roth insists that un- iversities should find ways to teach students transferable skills which they may rely on later at workplace, for example, introducing more project-based courses to enhance students’skills to solve real world problems.
