Week1：Summary Writing

Lesson 1-Writing Skill ：Summary Writing

Language Skills Cultivated ·Understanding of the original text ·Memorizing new vocabulary ·Sorting out information ·Organizing information What is a summary? Definition: A summary is an account in a reader’s own words that gives the main points of a piece of writing such as a newspaper article, a chapter of a book, or even a whole book. Priciple :in a reader’s own words What dose a summary look like? Three forms of summary 1.Summary sentence 2.Summary paragraph 3.Summary essay Summary sentence ·Witty ·Fun and insightful in a sense However ·NOT serious writing Even longer one-sentence summaries might still be too general to give the main idea of the author. Summary Paragraph Definition :A summary paragraph is a paragraph that summarizes the main idea of a piece of work. This form comes very handy when we quote someone else’s ideas in our own writing efforts. Summary writing Essential elements ·Introduction ·Body paragraphs ·Conclusion Summary essay 6 things ro do when writing a summary 1.Identify the title and author of the work. 2.State the thesis of the original article 3.Summarize the key supporting details 4.Restate the thesis in your conclusion 5.Paraphrase instead of copy;put" "wherever you quote more than five words from the text. 6.Keep the summary objective and fatual(do not include your own response) EXAMPLE Sample summary Introduction: Jeffrey J.Selingo in his article titled “What’s the Purpose of college: A job or an Education?” examines the disconnect/gap between what the society expects of a higher education and what the universities are really able to do. Though universities are struggling to introduce more partical majors to meet the needs of the job market, parents, students and employers are still dissatisfied. Selingo examines the cayses that have led to the problems and concludes that universities can do better to serve both a student’s need for a real education and a good preparation for their future job. Body paragraph 1: Michael Roth thinks three reasons have contributed to the general unhappiness about college education. First, sluggish economy makes middle class increasingly feel uncertain about their children’s future, so they expect too much of a college education. Second, soaring tuition fees raise both parents’ and students’ expectations of higher education. They want good value for money. Third, intensive media coverage has made the problem look bigger than it really is. For example, employers are surveyed monthly on their satisfaction with newly graduated employees. Conclusion: Therefore, universities can do better to give students a real education and at the same time make them better prepared for their jobs. How to write a summary? Strategy one - for an article that does not follow a recognizable outline. ·read the article and figue out the underlying logic around which the author develops the essay. For example, is it a cause and effect, or is it a classification, a procedure or a definition that is the major concern of the article? ·summarize the main ideas of the various parts of the article and make a list of these main ideas. ·reorganize the list of the main ideas. Group the sentences with similar ideas under a proper heading. Strategy two - for an article that has a clearly recognizable outline. ·Figure out the outline. ·Identify the main idea of each part. ·Write an introduction and a conclusion and use proper transitions to organize the main ideas according to the outline.
