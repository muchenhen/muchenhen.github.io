中国大学MOOC：学术英语写作基础Week 5 Lesson 2
2018-09-03|MOOC笔记mooc大学英语学术写作

Task 1 Useful Expressions

1.人们对登月反响的电视画面
Footage of the world’s reaction to the lunar landing
2.这一点从一开始就很清楚了。
The clues had been there from the start.
3.他们真正感叹的不是身下死灰色的星球，而是亮蓝色的地球。
Their real wonder was not at the dead gray planet beneath them, but at the vibrant blue globe in the distance.
4.带着更深的敬意
With a much deeper sense of reverence
5.好奇心永远得不到满足，它总是在寻求着新的目标。
Curiosity is never satisfied, it merely finds new targets.
6.最有天赋的宇航员
The most naturally gifted astronaut
7.成就本身并不重要，重要的追求的过程。
Achievements themselves aren’t what count, it’s the fact that you woeked for them.
8.感叹人类生活的多样性。
Marvel at the variety of human life.
9.往往到未知世界的探索却会人们更多地了解旅行者的家乡。
As so often, a journey into the unknown had revealed more about the traveler’s home than about the destination.
10.更好地了解了地球的脆弱
With a deep sense of the Earth’s fragility
11.你的观念发生了变化。
A shift takes place in your perception.
12.你的想法就会开始不同。
And you start to think quite differently.
13.这一发现极大地促进了地球上刚刚兴起的环保运动地发展。
The discovery gave a big boost to the nascent Green movement.
14.立足本地，放眼全球。
Think globally, act locally.
15.阿波罗倒计时结束升空的那一瞬间，就是全球化起步的那一刻。
T minus zero for Apollo was T plus one for globalization.
16.渴望新经历
Yearn for new experiences
17.我们对给我们泼冷水的人感到不满。
We resent anyone who dampens our excitement.
18.我向……表达了他的看法
He voiced his thoughts to
19.我意识到人类的本性就是不断地探索。
I realize there’s a fundamental truth to our nature: man must explore.
20.生活是个永恒的循环。
Life as a perpetual cycle.
21.概括了阿波罗计划的本质。
Sums up the very essence of the Apollo missions.
22.旅行不是你去过哪里，而是你发现自己。
Travel isn’t about where you’re going, it’s about who you are.
23.我们不能停止探索的脚步。
We shall not cease from exploration.
